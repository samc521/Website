QUICKSAND

Quicksand is a display sans serif with rounded terminals. The project was initiated by Andrew Paglinawan in 2008 using geometric shapes as a core foundation. It is designed for display purposes but kept legible enough to use in small sizes as well. In 2016, in collaboration with Andrew, it was thoroughly revised by Thomas Jockin to improve the quality. In 2019, Mirko Velimirovic converted the family into a variable font.

RESEARCH

A Initial research was essential to guarantee the success of the project. On this starting point, I was looking for answers to the following questions.

In which font Category quicksand is classified? Serif or Sans Serif? What are the characteristics of the font? What kind of message does it send? What kind of designs is the best fit for this font?

Using different websites and resources, such as Google Font I was able to determine that Quicksand is a sans serif typeface. It has Geometric parameters, rounded ends, and modern design. The font was created in 2008 by Andrew Paglinawan and since then it received three updates to improve font quality. This typeface can be considered a relatively new font and was created mainly to be used as a Display font. However, it is versatile and legible enough to use in small text sizes as well.

Based on the information gathered from my research, I was able to answers the three first questions mentioned above. However, to conclude this phase, I still needed to find what kind of designs was the best fit for the quicksand font. To find my last answer, I decided to test my typeface using various page layouts.

The rounded terminals along with the curvaceous design on this typeface create a feeling of new, approachable, and openness.
